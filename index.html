Kane Williamson is a New Zealand cricketer, widely regarded as one of the best batsmen in the world. Born in 1990, he made his international debut for New Zealand in 2010 and quickly established himself as a key player across all formats. Known for his calm demeanor, exceptional technique, and consistency, Williamson has been a standout performer for New Zealand, leading the team as captain in Test cricket and limited-overs formats. Under his leadership, New Zealand reached the finals of the ICC Cricket World Cup in 2015 and 2019, and won the ICC World Test Championship in 2021.

Joe Root is a renowned English cricketer, widely regarded as one of the best batsmen in the world. Born in 1990, he made his debut for England in 2012 and quickly became a key player in all formats of the game. Known for his consistency, technical skill, and versatility, Root has scored numerous centuries and played pivotal roles in many of England's major cricketing successes. He served as the captain of the England Test team, leading them through several challenging series. Root is celebrated for his cricketing intelligence and his ability to perform under pressure.

Steve Smith is an Australian cricketer, widely regarded as one of the best batsmen of his generation. Born in 1989, he initially started his career as a leg-spinner before transitioning into a top-order batsman. Known for his unorthodox but highly effective batting technique, Smith has been one of the most consistent run-scorers in international cricket. He has served as the captain of the Australian national team and led them to various successes, including a 2019 Ashes series win. Smith is known for his mental toughness, sharp cricketing acumen, and ability to perform in high-pressure situations.
